Balcón de Europa  ·  Nerja, Málaga Province

Nerja is the Costa del Sol done right — a cliff-top town that kept its soul while the resorts around it sold theirs. The centrepiece is the Balcón de Europa: a promenade jutting out above the sea, flanked by palm trees, with views along the coastline that make you stop walking and simply stare. Go at sunset. Tonight: drop the bags, find a terrace, order something cold.

The Alhambra  ·  Granada, Andalucía

Granada hits you immediately. The Alhambra palace complex sits above the city like something from another world — 700 years of Moorish architecture, intricate geometric tilework, and gardens fed by snowmelt from the Sierra Nevada. We have tickets booked for 5 August at 09:00. Day one is for the city itself: the Albaicín, Sacromonte, and Granada's extraordinary tapas culture where every drink comes with free food.

Alhambra — 5 August, 09:00 sharp for the Nasrid Palaces. This slot cannot be changed. We take the C3 bus from Plaza Nueva (5 min, €1.40). Bring your New Zealand passport — required at entry. Flat closed shoes essential — paths are cobbled and uneven. No selfie sticks or flash inside the palaces.

2 nights2h + stopsPuente Nuevo · El Tajo
Puente Nuevo  ·  El Tajo Gorge, Ronda

Stop at Setenil de las Bodegas on the drive — a village where houses are literally built under overhanging rock cliffs. Have coffee in a bar with a cliff for a ceiling. Then Ronda: a town on a plateau split by the El Tajo gorge, a sheer 100 metres deep. The Puente Nuevo bridge spans it. Ernest Hemingway lived here. Orson Welles had his ashes scattered nearby. Stand on that bridge, look straight down, and you'll understand why.

What to eat & drink

"Order what you don't recognise. Ask the waiter what they eat at home. That is always the right answer."

Gazpacho

Cold blended tomato soup. In August heat it is the most refreshing thing you will eat. Order it at every lunch, everywhere.

Jamón ibérico

Cured ham from acorn-fed pigs. Order a plate as a starter, every day. Nothing you have eaten at home is like this.

Pescaíto frito

Lightly fried fish — anchovies, squid, prawns. The Andalusian coastal specialty. Eat it at a chiringuito with cold beer.

Salmorejo

Granada's richer version of gazpacho — thick, topped with jamón and boiled egg. A full meal in a bowl. Order it in Granada.

Free tapas

In Granada, every drink comes with a free tapa. Two rounds of drinks equals two plates of food. Best deal in Europe.

Churros

Fried dough dipped in thick hot chocolate. For breakfast. Fieke and Matthijs will ask for them daily. Say yes every time.

On wine & sherry

Andalucía is sherry country. Ask for a chilled fino or manzanilla with tapas — local, inexpensive, pairs with everything. Rioja for evenings. Sangria is for tourists but on the beach, nobody will judge.

When to eat

Lunch at 14:00–16:00 is the main meal. Dinner starts at 21:00. Before 20:30, restaurants are empty or catering only to tourists. Eat when the locals eat — always better.

Flamenco

Andalucía is the birthplace of flamenco. In Sacromonte, Granada, you can watch it performed in the cave houses where it was born — raw, intense, nothing like a stage show. If you can do one thing in the evening in Granada, make it this.

Good to know

Spanish time

  • Breakfast 8–10:00 — coffee and tostada
  • Lunch 14:00–16:00 — the main meal
  • Siesta 14:00–17:00 — many shops close
  • Dinner 21:00+ — don't go before this
  • Sundays: many shops closed in the afternoon

August survival

  • SPF 50 every day — no exceptions
  • Sightsee before 10:00 or after 17:00
  • Carry water always — bars will refill for free
  • Hat, sunglasses, light linen clothing
  • Air-conditioning 13–17:00 is non-negotiable

Useful Spanish

  • "Por favor" — please
  • "Gracias" — thank you
  • "La cuenta, por favor" — the bill
  • "¿Habla inglés?" — do you speak English?
  • "Muy rico" — delicious. Use it constantly.
